3 Linear iBAG

This module fits the linear iBAG models proposed in Wang et al, 2013 and Jennings et al, 2013. The model fitting and outputs are shown in the Figure below.

iBAG model fitting: iBAG uses a hierarchical procedure by breaking the modeling into two parts, a mechanistic component (Step 1) that clarifies the molecular behaviors, mechanisms and relationships between and within the different types of molecular platforms. Subsequently, a clinical component (Step 2) that utilizes this information to assess associations with patient-specific clinical outcomes and/or phenotypes that characterize disease development or progression (e.g.Â survival times).

iBAG Model Outputs:

  • Posterior Probability Plots: Probability plots of the regression coefficients.

  • Gene Tables: Table of genes identified to have at least one significant marker by the above posterior probability plots.

  • Mechanistic Model Fits: A heatmap of driver genes.
